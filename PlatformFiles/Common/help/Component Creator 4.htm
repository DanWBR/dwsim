Black-Oil Fluid Definition

A black-oil fluid is a petroleum fluid (gas+oil+water) defined by a small set of properties which enables it to be used in a simulation together with the Black-Oil Property Package. Other property packages won't work with it because all the other required properties (critical, formation, molecular, T-dependent) will be missing from the fluid definition.


Black-Oil Properties

  1. Gas Specific Gravity
    Specific gravity of the gas at 60/60F or 20/4C.
     
  2. Oil Specific Gravity
    Specific gravity of the oil at 60/60F or 20/4C.
     
  3. Gas-Oil Ratio at Standard Conditions
    Volumetric ratio between gas and oil at standard conditions.
     
  4. Basic Sediments and Water
    Percentage of water in the liquid phase.
     
  5. Optional Data
    Only oil viscosity data is used at the moment, if informed.
